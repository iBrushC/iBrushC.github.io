About

Hi! My name is Andrew Combs, and as of writing this I'm a first year Electrical Engineering student at North Carolina State University. I am an avid maker and programmer, and love challenging myself with projects. My favorite projects generally involve simulations or creating sci-fi technology.

Outside of making stuff, I'm also a bread baker, chef, freerunner, chess player, 3D artist, and rock climber.

Skills

I've done at least one project in practically every major programming language, but I'm most confident with Python, JavaScript, C, C++, Rust, and a handful of shading languages.

On the hardware side, I'm experienced in circuit assembly and soldering, through hole and surface mount. I also know the ins and outs of 3D printing and am trained in shop safety.

I have been doing 3D art and rendering for well over seven years now, and can proficiently use Blender for artistic, scientific, and product rendering.

Projects

Robot Artist

I developed an algorithm to approximate an image with a series of penstrokes by combining ideas from image processing, genetic algoriths, and reinforcement learning. I then modified a 3D printer, using it to draw the created images. See it on github

Mechanical Dragon

I worked with a local elementary school to assemble, wire, and machine a six foot dragon for their production of "The Hobbit". The dragon was operated by a student through a software I wrote that would communicate with the dragon's microcontroller. On board the dragon was a custom written PID loop to move the mouth according to the lines. The system worked without fail for four performances!

Arbitrary Black Holes

I studied light transport, physics, and shaders to create and interactive simulation that can render black holes of any shape. It bends each ray of light according to the surface of the shape of the black hole, and can accurately replicate most notable phenomena.
